Buyer-Type Lens

Different buyer profiles produce materially different multiple ranges and synergy assumptions. Click to set lens.
Single-State Operator
1.0x – 1.5x rev
Smaller MA operator scaling up. Lower multiples, conservative EBITDA lens, minimal synergies. Often the slowest/lowest-priced path.
MA At-Cap Acquirer
1.5x – 2.0x rev
MA operator currently at the 3-store cap, motivated by cap-lift to expand. Strategic EBITDA lens. Moderate synergies (overlap on roles, suppliers).
MSO (out-of-state)
0.8x – 1.2x rev
National multi-state operator entering MA. Cannot continue 471(c) — pays on a 280E-bound EBITDA, structurally lower. Pays for license + footprint.
ESOP Buyer
1.5x – 2.0x rev
Employee stock ownership plan buyer. Different value drivers (governance, employee continuity, tax treatment). Cap-exempt under §16(b)(i).

Methodology Notes & Sources

TTM Revenue ($8,810,261). Source: New England Craft Cultivators LLC 2025 Profit and Loss by Month, accrual basis, year-ended Dec 31, 2025. Reflects fully-integrated NECC + MACC since Jan 1, 2025.

Forward 12-month revenue ($10,740,000). Composed of Dracut $5.82M + Pepperell $2.97M + Groton $750K + Colrain wholesale $1.2M, reflecting full-year run-rate at Groton maturity and the manufacturing license activation expected September 2026.

Adjusted EBITDA — Strategic ($1,968,186). Tree House internal calculation reflecting strategic-buyer synergy assumptions (categorized owner-comp absorption plus non-comp synergies). Underlying line-item workpaper documents each add-back.

Adjusted EBITDA — Mid (~$1,650,000). Working estimate: Strategic lens with partial synergy realization. Refine during line-item workpaper review.

Adjusted EBITDA — Conservative (~$1,300,000). Operating income plus interest reclassification from COGS, plus estimated D&A, with minimal additional adjustments. Represents the floor view a financial buyer or first-pass diligence would compute. Depreciation add-back is approximate pending the formal 2025 D&A schedule.

Revenue multiple range. 1.0x–2.0x reflects the consolidated MA cannabis comp range observed across recent transactions. MSO buyers anchor lower (0.8x–1.2x) due to 280E carry-forward considerations. Cap-lift acquirers pay premium (1.5x–2.0x). EBITDA multiples used in cross-check (3.0x–4.0x) reflect the same comp set translated to the EBITDA frame.

Cap-lift legislative source. Chapter 65 of the Acts of 2026, signed April 2026. §16 rewritten by §36 of the act: cap = 6 retailers / 3 manufacturers / 3 cultivators, with sub-20%/no-control and ESOP exemptions. §60 stages the rollout: non-social-equity licensees limited to 5 retail licenses for the first 12 months of new applications; 6th license available after that 12-month period. CCC must begin accepting applications within 2 months of effective date.

Sister-entity scenario. Speculative read of §16(b)(ii). Statute exempts sub-20%/no-control passive equity from cap aggregation. Whether same-controllers can run two licensee entities while staying below the control threshold is a counsel-advice question. Flagged speculative until pressure-tested with outside counsel.

Asset floor. Inventory $1,470,721 from 2025 year-end balance sheet, haircut to 80–90% per going-concern convention. Cash $354,316 + AR $161,723 at 85%. FF&E + leasehold improvements at discount to book. Retail and cultivation license values estimated from MA market — refine during Verticalization page build for the cultivation portion.

Synergy add-back assumptions. Default values are illustrative and subject to refinement. The Strategic lens assumes a buyer who already has CFO, GC, COO infrastructure — typical of MA at-cap acquirers and ESOP buyers. Conservative lens assumes financial buyer with no existing infrastructure. Replacement-cost benchmarks should ultimately be sourced from a cannabis-specific compensation study.

Comps data file. Public MSO multiples and MA private transactions are loaded from dashboard/data/comps-data.js at page load. Refresh by editing that file directly — no HTML changes needed. The file's header comment documents refresh cadence (quarterly for public MSOs after each earnings cycle; monthly + ad hoc for MA private). Each MA private row carries an explicit confidence rating and source citation.

Other data freshness. Revenue, balance sheet, and EBITDA inputs are point-in-time as of Apr 2026. Live wiring to Dutchie POS for daily revenue and to QuickBooks for post-monthly-close financials is a future iteration.
