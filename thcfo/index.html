Strategic Context · April 2026

Macro environment and inflection points the dashboard responds to.
Cap-Lift Legislation

Chapter 65 of Acts of 2026

Massachusetts retail cap moves from 3 stores per company to 5 stores (effective late June 2026 for non-social-equity licensees) and 6 stores (effective late June 2027). Sub-20% non-control passive equity and ESOP-owned licensees are exempt from the cap. Tree House becomes a candidate to expand from 3 to 5 retail locations during the next 14 months, with a 6th available the year after. Buyer-pool composition expands materially: every MA operator currently at the cap becomes a potential acquirer, plus MSOs and ESOP buyers.
Deleveraging Complete

Institutional Debt Retired

FY 2025 saw retirement of five institutional facilities (Cobalt, Cromwell, EBF, FinCoast, and reduction of Sky Acquisitions) totaling $876K of principal. Replaced in part with $381K of friendlier investor notes at flexible terms. Net deleveraging of ~$524K. Post 12/31, EBF Holdings paid in full and Massachusetts DOR sales-tax payable largely resolved. Result: 4.15x DSCR on Strategic EBITDA, no refinancing risk in the 36-month horizon, and substantial unused borrowing capacity for cap-lift-driven expansion.
Vertical Integration

Farm Fully Consolidated

MA Craft Cultivation became a wholly-owned subsidiary effective January 1, 2025, completing the absorption of pre-acquisition non-NECC owners. Books are fully integrated from that date forward. The cultivation operation contributes ~$931K of annual economic value across margin uplift on own-grown product through retail, wholesale revenue to other dispensaries, and exchange/trade transactions. Manufacturing license activation expected September 2026 — first opportunity to internalize pre-roll and infused-pre-roll production at higher per-unit revenue than bulk flower.
Tax Strategy

§471(c) Through Year 3

Effective federal tax for FY 2024 was approximately zero, the result of three consecutive years of §471(c) inventory accounting under professional tax counsel. The strategy capitalizes operating costs into inventoriable cost rather than treating them as ordinary opex (which §280E prevents from deducting). Implication for valuation conversations: an MSO buyer above the §471(c) threshold cannot continue the strategy post-close, which is itself a structural argument for revenue-multiple framing rather than EBITDA-multiple framing in any sale negotiation.
